# Product

## Register

product

## Users

HR and L&D leaders, learning buyers, program managers, and individual change
practitioners at organizations going through change. They arrive with a real,
often messy situation in their head ("we're rolling out a new system and adoption
is stalling") rather than a course name. Their context is evaluative and
time-pressured: they need to know which Prosci program actually addresses their
challenge so they can act or make a recommendation upward, not browse a catalog.

The job to be done: "When I'm facing a specific change challenge, I want to
describe it naturally and instantly see which Prosci program will actually help,
so I can act with confidence instead of guessing from a catalog."

## Product Purpose

An LLM-powered course finder for Prosci, the global leader in change management
training and certification. A user describes a change challenge in plain English;
the system returns a short synthesized AI recommendation followed by a ranked set
of matching Prosci courses, each with an explicit "why this fits" rationale and
controls to refine.

Success is **confident course selection**: a user moves from a vague challenge to
decisively choosing and acting on the right Prosci program. The finder is judged
on whether it produces that confident decision, not on time-on-page or breadth of
browsing.

## Brand Personality

Approachable and warm, on a foundation of trusted, credible, and confident.
The experience should feel like a knowledgeable, human guide that lowers the
intimidation of enterprise change management, conversational and encouraging
without sacrificing authority. Editorial and human, never corporate-cold.

Three words: **warm, credible, confident.**

Voice: plain-spoken and reassuring. Explain reasoning the way a good consultant
would, in the user's language, not in catalog taxonomy. Emotional goal: the user
feels understood and capable, relieved to have found the right answer.

## Anti-references

- **Corporate-cold SaaS.** No navy/blue enterprise-dashboard feel, no sterile
  density. The retired Prosci navy `#0B2545` / blue `#1F5BD0` / orange `#F26522`
  palette and Inter type are explicitly off the table.
- **Catalog / search-engine grid.** Never an impersonal results grid that dumps
  courses with no reasoning. Every result carries a rationale; the page should
  feel curated and advised, not like e-commerce listing pages.
- Secondary cautions (from the visual system): avoid generic AI-chatbot bareness
  and flashy/gimmicky animation that would undercut credibility.

## Design Principles

1. **Reasoning is the product.** Every recommendation explains why it fits. A
   ranked list without rationale fails the brief, no matter how accurate.
2. **Describe, don't navigate.** Meet users in natural language. Plain-English
   input and human explanation beat filters, taxonomies, and catalog structure.
3. **Warm authority.** Lower the intimidation of change management with an
   approachable, human tone, backed visibly by Prosci's credibility and expertise.
4. **Confidence is the deliverable.** Optimize every screen for a decisive choice,
   not for browsing or dwell time. Reduce, don't expand, the user's options.
5. **Restraint signals trust.** Airy, editorial, color used sparingly. Calm beats
   flashy; polish that draws attention to itself erodes the credibility this brand
   trades on.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**. Standard for professional, enterprise B2B audiences:
- AA contrast on all text and meaningful UI. Watch magenta `#FF1AA3` on cream and
  on white, it is the brand's key-action color but is borderline for AA on small
  text; reserve it for large text, icons, and accents, and verify any text use.
- Full keyboard operability with a visible focus state (the brand's soft magenta
  focus ring satisfies this if contrast-checked).
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`: the fade/slide-up result reveals and decorative
  color blurs must degrade to no motion.
- Do not rely on color alone to signal match strength; pair tint with text/label.
